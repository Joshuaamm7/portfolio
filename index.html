About Me

My interest in performance started with sports — basketball and F1 — where I was always drawn to the strategy and decisions behind outcomes. Over time, that curiosity evolved into using data to understand complex systems and improve decisions, which is what led me to data science.

Most recently, I led a team of five at the Center for Energy Research at UCSD, analyzing how EV adoption impacts oil imports and building tools that turned that analysis into something executives could actually use. I also built an NBA G-League call-up predictor whose model outputs were later validated by an NBA scout — probably the most satisfying real-world feedback I've gotten on a project so far.

Outside of academics, I enjoy photography and spending time with my cat.
